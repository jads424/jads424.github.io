Untitled

In my mind,

I am ten feet tall and crimson-wreathed,

Virile and bursting with mealy juice like ripe corn.

With blood running from my temples to my lips,

I see eye to eye with the dragons and the beasts.

Outside,

The corn is withered to papery husks,

And the dragons lie shriveled in the dust,

And to imagine fighting them is pathetic,

Like stabbing someone who is already dead.

In my mind,

My sword cuts straight and true,

Slicing through muscle and sinew and bone

And lies and shadows and constructions,

Spraying the blood of monsters and monuments.

Outside,

I am the only one weaker than the one I fight.

Only once I’ve slain the demon

Do I realize he was an angel watching me,

And I’ve reduced my army by one.

In my mind,

Chivalry and manhood are effortless.

Like a rich man tossing pennies to the hordes,

I am never diminished by my charity;

I do not diminish myself when others see me as I am.

And in my mind,

I plunge into blue waters,

Down to silent, sunless depths,

To reemerge:

Whole fields of wildflowers blooming all at once,

Light refracting off every petal,

Blinding and indecent in my innocent glory,

Bursting back to life.

Rinsed clean and scrubbed to my essence,

I reemerge, a thousand times a day,

If I care to.

And if I could pass freely through the door,

Then perhaps I could be me in your reality,

Instead of a stranger in my own.
